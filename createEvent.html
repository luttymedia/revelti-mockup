Ticket Creation Guide

Category

Assign a category to your ticket (e.g., Main Passes, Accommodation, VIP) to automatically group related tickets together on the public purchase page, creating an organized and seamless attendee experience.

Ticket Status

A ticket's status controls its visibility and availability on your public event page.

  • On Sale: The ticket is live and can be purchased immediately.
  • Draft: The ticket is saved in the system but is hidden from the public. Use this to prepare tickets in advance.
  • Scheduled: The ticket will automatically become "On Sale" at a future date you specify.

Commission Fee Paid By

Choose who absorbs the platform's commission fee for this ticket type.

  • Attendee: The commission fee is added on top of the ticket price and paid by the attendee at checkout.
  • Organizer: The attendee pays the exact ticket price, and the commission fee is deducted from your final payout.
  • 50/50 Split: The commission fee is split evenly between the attendee and the organizer.

Pricing Strategy

Choose how your ticket's price is determined.

  • Simple Price: The ticket has one fixed price that does not change.
  • Dynamic Pricing: The ticket price changes automatically based on rules you set. This is perfect for early-bird discounts or creating urgency.

Dynamic Pricing — Date-Based Rules

When you select Dynamic Pricing, you set a Base Price and then add Pricing Rules. Each rule defines a future price increase by date.

Example:

Base Price: €170.00

Rule 1: Increase price to €190.00 when Date is after > Sep 1, 2025

Rule 2: Increase price to €220.00 when Date is after > Oct 1, 2025

The ticket will cost €170 initially, then automatically jump to €190 on September 2nd, and finally to €220 on October 2nd.

Dynamic Pricing — Sales-Based Rules

You can also set rules that trigger a price change when a certain number of tickets have been sold.

Base Price: €10.00

Rule 1: Increase price to €15.00 when 20+ tickets sold

Rule 2: Increase price to €20.00 when 30+ tickets sold

This creates natural urgency — as demand grows, the price increases automatically.

Display Final Price (Strikethrough)

This feature shows a higher, "final" price with a strikethrough next to the current price, highlighting the discount for buyers (e.g., €220.00 €190.00).

  • By default, the system automatically uses the highest price from your dynamic rules as the final price.
  • For marketing, you can check the box to manually enter a different, often higher, price to show an even bigger discount.

Shared Capacity Pool

Group multiple ticket types into a Shared Capacity Pool so they draw from the same inventory limit. Sales of any ticket in the pool count toward this shared limit — ideal for tiered pricing or linked accommodations.

  • Select 2 or more tickets using the checkboxes in Step 3
  • Click Group into Shared Capacity Pool
  • Set a shared limit — all tickets in the pool will respect this cap

Venue Capacity Bar

The venue capacity bar gives you a real-time view of how many tickets you've allocated versus the physical venue limit. Enter a venue capacity in Step 3 and the bar will update automatically as you add or adjust ticket quantities.

If allocated tickets exceed venue capacity, a red warning is shown.

Checkout Override

By default, every attendee answers the same set of checkout questions (Full Name, Email, etc.).

If a specific ticket type needs different information, enable Override Checkout Questions on that ticket. The questions will then be specific to that ticket only.

Purchase Constraint

Control the quantity rules buyers must follow when adding this ticket to their order.

  • None: No restriction — buyers can purchase any number of tickets individually.
  • Grouped (fixed pack): Tickets must be purchased in exact multiples of a fixed group size. Ideal for hotel rooms, couple passes, or table bookings (e.g. group size of 2 means buyers must purchase 2, 4, 6… tickets at a time).
  • Minimum quantity: Buyers must add at least a set number of tickets in a single order. Any quantity above the minimum is allowed (e.g. minimum 10 means buyers can purchase 10, 11, 12…). Useful for group discounts.

The two modes are mutually exclusive — a ticket can only use one at a time. Both modes are fully compatible with dynamic pricing, shared capacity pools, and campaign codes.
